# Lead Magnets

Expert knowledge for creating, designing, and optimizing lead magnets that attract high-quality leads and convert visitors into email subscribers.

## What Makes a Good Lead Magnet

A lead magnet must satisfy three criteria simultaneously:

1. **Specific:** Solves one specific problem, not everything
2. **Instantly valuable:** Value is felt immediately, not over time
3. **Positioned for sale:** The person who wants this lead magnet is exactly the person who should buy your product

The third criterion is the one most teams get wrong. A generic checklist builds a list. A positioned checklist builds a pipeline.

## Lead Magnet Type Selection

| Type | Best for | Time to create | Lead quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| Checklist / cheat sheet | Specific process, quick reference | Low | Medium |
| Template | Recurring task, saves time | Medium | High |
| Calculator / tool | ROI or sizing decisions | High | Very high |
| Mini-course (email) | Complex topic, education | High | High |
| Report / research | Industry authority building | High | Medium |
| Swipe file | Examples and inspiration | Low | Medium |
| Webinar / workshop | Live education, warm nurture | Medium | High |
| Free trial / freemium | Product-led | Low | Very high (if used) |
| Audit / assessment | Diagnostic, high-touch | Medium | Very high |

**Type selection rules:**
- Buyer in evaluation mode → calculator or comparison template
- Buyer in education mode → guide or mini-course
- Buyer has a recurring operational task → template or checklist
- Trying to segment by pain point → assessment

## Value-to-Ask Ratio

The value the prospect perceives must exceed the friction of the ask.

**Friction factors:**
- Email only: Low friction
- Email + name: Medium friction
- Email + name + company + phone: High friction
- Long form with intent questions: Very high friction

**Match friction to value:**
- Checklist or cheat sheet → Email only
- Template or calculator → Email + name
- Report or mini-course → Email + name + company
- Audit or 1:1 consultation → Full form

**Rule:** Only ask for information you will actually use in your follow-up sequence.

## Lead Magnet Creation Process

### Step 1: Define the ICP and trigger moment

Answer these before touching the content:
- Who is this for? (Specific role, company type, pain state)
- What are they trying to do right now? (Trigger moment)
- What's one specific problem this helps them solve?
- What would they search for to find this?

### Step 2: Write the "before/after" promise

Articulate the transformation:
- **Before:** [Painful state they're in]
- **After:** [Specific state they achieve using this resource]

This promise is the headline of your landing page.

Example:
- Before: "Spending hours every week manually pulling campaign data from 5 different tools"
- After: "One dashboard showing all your campaign metrics — set up in 20 minutes"

### Step 3: Create the content

**For checklists:**
- 10-25 items max
- Group into 3-5 sections
- Make each item actionable (start with a verb)
- Include examples where a step could be ambiguous

**For templates:**
- Design for immediate use — minimal customization required
- Include instructions or a sample filled version
- Use common tools (Google Sheets, Notion, Word)
- Make the template work for the most common use case, not all use cases

**For calculators:**
- Input cells clearly labeled with helper text
- Output highlighted prominently
- Include explanation of the formula/methodology

**For mini-courses (email):**
- 5-7 emails, one lesson per email
- Each email teaches one actionable thing
- Emails sent daily or every other day
- Final email transitions to a product offer

**For reports:**
- Original data wins over curated data
- Include methodology section
- Use visualizations (charts > tables for most data)
- Include 3-5 key insights upfront (executive summary)

## Landing Page Copy for Lead Magnets

### Headline Formula

"[Get/Download/Access] [Specific resource] to [Specific outcome] [Time qualifier or without sacrifice]"

Examples:
- "Download the SaaS churn prevention checklist — 47 tactics used by 500+ companies"
- "Get the free template: Monthly marketing report ready in 30 minutes"
- "Free calculator: See exactly how much [tool] could save your team"

### Body Copy Structure (short form)

1. What you get (2-3 bullet points — specific, not vague)
2. Who this is for (one line — qualification signals)
3. Social proof (number of downloads, testimonial, or logo)
4. Email form + CTA button

**Bullet point format:**
- "How to [do specific thing] — [specific outcome or time saving]"
- "[Specific template] for [specific use case]"
- "The [specific method] that [company type] use to [achieve result]"

### Thank You Page (don't waste it)

The thank you page is the highest-intent moment in the subscriber journey. Use it to:
- Deliver the lead magnet (or confirm delivery)
- Offer a low-commitment next step (watch a demo video, read a case study)
- For high-velocity products: offer a free trial or coupon

## Confirmation Email Sequence

### Email 1 (immediate): Delivery
- Subject: "Here's your [lead magnet name]"
- Body: Download link + one sentence reminder of the value
- CTA: One — open/download the resource

### Email 2 (Day 1-2): Value amplifier
- Subject: "The one thing most people miss in [topic]"
- Body: One insight that makes the lead magnet more useful
- CTA: Read a related piece of content

### Email 3 (Day 3-4): Social proof / product bridge
- Subject: "How [company type] used [lead magnet topic] to [result]"
- Body: Mini case study. Bridge from the resource to your product.
- CTA: Start free trial / book demo / see how [product] automates this

## Lead Quality vs Volume Tradeoff

Higher friction → fewer leads → higher quality.

**When to optimize for volume:**
- Building brand awareness and list size
- Sales motion is low-touch / self-serve
- Lead magnet is top-of-funnel (educational)

**When to optimize for quality:**
- Enterprise sales with human follow-up
- Product requires significant evaluation

**Quality signal: Activated lead rate**
What % of lead magnet subscribers take a meaningful product action within 30 days? If < 5%, the lead magnet is attracting the wrong audience.

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Any lead magnet is better than none — let's just make something" | A mispositioned lead magnet fills your list with non-buyers. They waste nurture budget and distort metrics. |
| "We need to cover everything in the guide — make it comprehensive" | Comprehensive guides are not lead magnets. They're content. Lead magnets solve one specific problem immediately. |
| "We'll build the sequence later — just capture the email first" | 70% of lead magnet subscribers disengage within 72 hours. The immediate sequence is what converts them. |
| "We need a beautiful design before we launch" | A plain Google Doc template with useful content outperforms a beautifully designed checklist with weak content. |
| "Everyone will want this resource" | "Everyone" means "no one." The more specific the ICP and trigger, the higher the conversion rate. |
