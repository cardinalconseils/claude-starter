# Social Content

Expertise in building social content that grows audiences and converts them — not content that fills a calendar. Every post has a job (educate, prove, humanize, convert). Content without a job is noise.

## Platform Selection by Audience

Not all platforms are worth the effort:

- **Twitter/X:** Founder voice, real-time commentary, B2B thought leadership. Threads > single tweets for reach. 1-2 hashtags max. 280 character limit per post.
- **LinkedIn:** B2B sales, hiring, investor visibility. The algorithm rewards dwell time. 210 chars above fold before "see more" — that's your hook. Up to 3,000 characters per post.
- **Instagram:** Visual products, lifestyle brands, B2C. Reels > static for reach. Hook in first 3 seconds. Caption up to 2,200 characters.
- **TikTok/Reels:** Educational entertainment. Hook in first 3 seconds. 60 seconds ideal length. Authentic beats produced.

**Simple rule:**
- B2B audience: LinkedIn + Twitter/X
- B2C audience: Instagram + TikTok/Reels
- Don't spread across all four without a full content team

## Content Pillar Ratios (30-Day Calendar)

Build a 30-day calendar with these 5 pillars. The ratios prevent failure modes.

| Pillar | Ratio | Posts | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Education | 40% | 12/month | Teach something useful. Build authority. |
| Proof | 20% | 6/month | Case studies, results, testimonials, before/after. |
| Culture | 20% | 6/month | Team, process, behind-the-scenes. Builds liking. |
| Engagement | 10% | 3/month | Questions, polls, opinion posts. Drives comments. |
| Promotion | 10% | 3/month | Direct product mention, offer, or CTA. |

Over-promoting (> 10%) → audience disengages. Under-promoting (0%) → lots of engagement, no revenue. Too polished → low algorithmic reach. Culture content humanizes and boosts organic reach.

## Hook Writing

Every post lives or dies on its first line. The hook determines whether anyone reads the rest.

**Hook formulas:**

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| Curiosity | "Nobody talks about this, but [insight]" |
| List | "7 things I learned building [X] to [milestone]:" |
| Contrarian | "Hot take: [thing everyone does] is actually killing your [outcome]" |
| Story | "I made a $50K mistake last year. Here's what happened:" |
| Question | "What would you do if your best customer churned tomorrow?" |
| Outcome | "We went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe]. Here's how:" |

**Hooks that kill posts:**
- "In today's post..." — visibility death
- "I wanted to share..." — nobody cares that you wanted to
- "Excited to announce..." — only your team cares
- Starting with "We" — company voice, not human voice

## Platform-Specific Best Practices

### LinkedIn
- First 210 characters must stand alone — the hook must work before "see more"
- Line breaks between every 2-3 sentences — walls of text get skipped
- End with a question to drive comments (comments boost algorithmic reach)
- Best time: Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9am or 12-1pm in your audience's timezone
- Native video outperforms YouTube links
- Carousel PDFs generate high saves and shares

### Twitter/X
- Threads outperform single tweets for engagement and reach
- Thread format: Hook tweet → 5-10 insight tweets → CTA tweet (follow, link, or retweet)
- 1-2 hashtags max — more looks spammy
- Reply to comments within the first hour (signals algorithm value)
- Best time: weekdays 8-10am

### Instagram
- Caption hook must match the visual hook — both must stop the scroll
- Carousel posts (swipe) get 3× more reach than static posts
- Reels algorithm favors content that gets rewatched (keep them under 30s for most audiences)
- Hashtags: 5-10 relevant ones, not 30 generic ones
- Stories maintain audience warmth; feed posts build audience

### TikTok/Reels
- First 3 seconds must hook — use text overlay + movement
- Native-feel content beats produced content for cold audiences
- Pattern: hook → tension → payoff (educational or entertaining)
- Add text captions (60%+ of views are without sound)

## Content Creation Templates

### Education Post (LinkedIn/Twitter)
```
[Hook — contrarian claim or outcome statement]

Here's the framework I use:

1. [First insight — surprising or specific]
2. [Second insight — builds on first]
3. [Third insight — the key unlock]

The mistake most people make: [common wrong approach]

The fix: [your approach]

[Question to drive comments or CTA]
```

### Social Proof Post
```
[Customer name or type] went from [specific before state] to [specific after state].

Here's what changed:

[The specific thing that made the difference]

[Quote from customer or specific metric]

[Low-friction CTA: "DM me if you want the full breakdown"]
```

### Culture Post
```
[First-person story opener — something real that happened]

[What surprised you about it]

[The lesson you took from it]

[Question or invitation to share their experience]
```

## Brand Voice on Social

Adapt your voice to platform norms while staying consistent:

- **Formal brand on LinkedIn:** OK to be less formal than emails — still professional but conversational
- **Bold/contrarian brand:** Use the contrarian hook formula freely, take positions
- **Emotional/story-driven brand:** Lead with a person or situation, not a stat

Social is where brand voice is most visible. Inconsistency between platforms is jarring.

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|---|---|
| "We post when we have something to say" | Algorithms reward consistency. Irregular posting gets deprioritized. |
| "Our content gets engagement but no sales" | Engagement without conversion means missing the 10% promotion pillar. Add it. |
| "LinkedIn is too formal for our brand" | LinkedIn rewards authentic > formal. The most viral LinkedIn content reads like a personal story, not a press release. |
| "We need better design before we post" | Text posts on LinkedIn outperform graphics. Words > design on most platforms for B2B. |
| "We need to be on all platforms" | One platform done well beats four platforms done poorly. Start with one, dominate it, then expand. |
