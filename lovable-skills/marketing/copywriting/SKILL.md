# Copywriting

Expertise in writing copy that converts — not copy that sounds good. The difference: converting copy is written for a specific reader at a specific awareness level with a single desired action. Generic copy is written for everyone and converts no one.

## Framework Selection

Choose the right framework for the context:

- **Cold audience, pain-driven → PAS:** Lead with the problem they already feel. Problem → Agitate → Solution.
- **Solution-aware, any format → AIDA:** Hook them, build desire, one CTA. Attention → Interest → Desire → Action.
- **Transformation / testimonial → BAB:** Before is where they are, after is where they want to be. Before → After → Bridge.
- **Feature announcement → FAB:** Features are facts, benefits are why they care. Feature → Advantage → Benefit.
- **Long-form / high-ticket → 4Ps:** Picture the scenario, promise the outcome, prove it works, push to act.

## Brand Voice Adaptation

Before writing, establish tone on these axes (1=left, 5=right):

- **Formal ←→ Casual (1-5):**
  - Score ≤ 2: full sentences, no contractions, no slang
  - Score ≥ 4: contractions OK, conversational asides, shorter sentences

- **Rational ←→ Emotional (1-5):**
  - Score ≤ 2: lead with data, outcomes quantified, no adjectives
  - Score ≥ 4: sensory language, identity-based framing ("people like you"), metaphors

- **Conservative ←→ Bold (1-5):**
  - Score ≤ 2: expected benefit claims, safe CTAs ("Learn more")
  - Score ≥ 4: pattern-interrupt headlines, contrarian claims (back with proof)

If tone hasn't been defined: ask the user for the three axis scores before writing.

## The PAS Framework (Cold Audiences)

**Problem:** Name the pain they already feel — in their language, not yours.
> "Your reporting takes 4 hours every Monday."

**Agitate:** Intensify the pain by showing consequences.
> "That's 200 hours a year that could go toward strategy, hiring, or growth — spent in spreadsheets instead."

**Solution:** Present the product as the natural fix.
> "[Product] pulls every metric into one dashboard. Monday reporting: 15 minutes."

## The AIDA Framework (Solution-Aware Audiences)

**Attention:** Stop the scroll with a specific, relevant hook.
**Interest:** Connect to their situation — why does this matter to them specifically?
**Desire:** Paint the after state — what life looks like when the problem is solved.
**Action:** One CTA, specific, with the friction reducer below it.

## The BAB Framework (Transformation Stories)

**Before:** "We used to spend 3 days pulling our monthly report."
**After:** "Now it takes 20 minutes."
**Bridge:** "[Product] automated the data layer. Here's how."

Use BAB for testimonials, case study copy, and email sequences.

## Conversion Quality Signals

Copy likely to convert:
- Headline answers "what's in it for me" in ≤ 7 words
- Reader can identify themselves in the first sentence
- One CTA, not three
- Benefit stated before feature

Copy that won't convert:
- "We are [company], we do [thing]" openers — reader doesn't care yet
- Adjectives without proof ("world-class", "best-in-class", "revolutionary")
- CTA that says "Submit" or "Click here"
- Social proof missing or buried below fold

## Headline Formulas

**Outcome-specific:**
- "[Specific result] in [specific timeframe]"
- "How [company/person like them] got [result] without [sacrifice]"

**Problem-first:**
- "Still [painful problem]?"
- "The real reason [ICP] can't [achieve goal]"

**Identity-based:**
- "For [specific role] who [specific situation]"
- "Built for teams that [specific behavior]"

**Proof-led:**
- "[Number] [ICP] switched to [product] this month"
- "[Customer name] went from [before] to [after]"

## CTA Writing Rules

Start with a verb. Be specific about what happens next. Add a friction reducer.

| Weak CTA | Strong CTA |
|---|---|
| Submit | Start your free 14-day trial |
| Learn more | See how it works in 3 minutes |
| Click here | Get your free report |
| Sign up | Create your account — no credit card needed |

## Copy by Format

**Hero copy (above the fold):**
- H1: The specific outcome for the specific ICP (≤ 10 words)
- Subheadline: How you deliver it + key differentiator (1-2 sentences)
- CTA: Verb + what happens + friction reducer
- Trust signal below CTA: logo bar, rating, or user count

**Ad copy (Meta/Google):**
- Hook in first line — problem or outcome, never "Introducing..."
- One idea per ad, not three
- Body: problem → bridge → CTA (under 125 words for Meta primary text)
- 3 headline variants per placement, test hook style first

**Email body:**
- Subject line: specific, under 50 characters, no clickbait
- Preview text: complements subject, doesn't repeat it
- Opening: acknowledge their situation before pitching anything
- One ask per email — link to the next step, don't offer five

**Landing page:**
- Every section = one message, one supporting proof, one CTA
- Testimonials must be specific: name + company + measurable outcome
- Pricing section: anchored (show highest tier first or recommended plan)

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|---|---|
| "We'll write copy after the product is done" | Copy IS the product for the first 90 days. It determines whether anyone clicks to see the product. |
| "Our audience is sophisticated — they want detail, not short copy" | Short copy fails when it's vague. Long copy fails when it's padded. Length follows complexity of the ask. |
| "PAS feels too salesy" | PAS is just empathy structured: "I see your problem, here's why it matters, here's what fixes it." |
| "We can't A/B test copy yet" | Write 3 headline variants by default. Pick one to run now, keep the others for later. Cost is minutes. |
