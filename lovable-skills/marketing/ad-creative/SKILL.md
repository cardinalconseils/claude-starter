# Ad Creative

Expert knowledge for writing, iterating, and scaling ad creative that drives clicks, conversions, and ROAS across paid advertising platforms.

## Core Principle

Ad creative is a headline delivery machine. The job of every element is to earn the next read. Hook → Interest → Desire → Action.

## Hook Formulas (First 3 Seconds)

The hook determines whether anyone reads the rest. Spend 50% of creative effort here.

**Problem-first hooks:**
- "Still [painful problem]?"
- "Why does [undesired outcome] keep happening?"
- "[Number] mistakes [ICP] make with [topic]"

**Outcome-first hooks:**
- "[Specific result] in [specific timeframe] — no [common objection]"
- "How [company/person like them] got [result] without [sacrifice]"
- "The [adjective] way to [desirable outcome]"

**Curiosity hooks:**
- "Nobody talks about this, but [insight]"
- "The real reason [ICP] can't [achieve goal]"
- "[Counterintuitive claim] (here's why)"

**Social proof hooks:**
- "[Number] [ICP] switched to [product] this month"
- "[Customer name] went from [before] to [after] using [product]"

## Headline Structures by Platform

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
- **Character limits:** Headline 40 chars, Primary text 125 chars visible, Description 30 chars
- **Best structures:** Benefit-first ("Cut reporting time by 80%"), Question, Social proof
- **Format tip:** Short primary text (2-3 sentences) often beats long-form for cold audiences

### Google Search Ads
- **Character limits:** 3 headlines × 30 chars, 2 descriptions × 90 chars
- **Best structures:** Include keyword in headline 1, benefit in headline 2, CTA in headline 3
- **Match intent:** Mirror the user's search language back

### LinkedIn
- **Character limits:** Headline 70 chars, Body 600 chars
- **Audience:** Professional context — lead with business outcome and credibility
- **Best structures:** "[Job title] at [company type]: [specific outcome]"

### TikTok / Video
- **Hook:** First 1-2 seconds must stop the scroll — text overlay + visual motion
- **Formats:** Talking head, product demo, UGC-style, before/after, list/tutorial
- **Length:** 15-30 seconds for prospecting; 30-60 for warm audiences

## Creative Testing Matrix

Never test random variants. Build a structured testing matrix:

| Dimension | Variants to Test |
|---|---|
| Hook style | Problem-first vs outcome-first vs social proof |
| Offer framing | Free trial vs demo vs case study vs pricing |
| Audience angle | Pain-focused vs aspiration-focused |
| Format | Static vs video vs carousel |
| CTA | "Start free" vs "See how it works" vs "Get a demo" |

Test one dimension at a time.

## UGC-Style Creative

User-generated content style (even when produced professionally) typically outperforms polished creative for cold audiences because it feels authentic.

**UGC formula:**
1. "I've been using [product] for [timeframe] and here's what happened..."
2. [Specific before state — painful and relatable]
3. [Specific turning point — the feature that changed things]
4. [Specific result with numbers]
5. "If you're [ICP with problem], this is worth trying."

**UGC production tips:**
- Phone-recorded video > studio video for UGC feel
- Direct eye contact with camera
- Natural speech with small hesitations
- Specific numbers beat vague claims

## Creative Brief Template

Before writing, answer:
- **Product:** What does it do?
- **ICP:** Who is this ad for? (job title, company size, pain state)
- **One job:** What should this ad make them do? (click, sign up, request demo)
- **Key benefit:** "[Product] helps [ICP] [achieve outcome] without [sacrifice]"
- **Proof point:** One specific, credible data point
- **Objection to handle:** The one reason they won't click
- **Platform:** Where will this run?
- **Audience stage:** Cold/warm/retargeting?

## Creative Fatigue Detection

Signals:
- CTR declining week-over-week (> 20% drop over 2 weeks)
- Cost per click rising without budget changes
- Frequency > 3-4 for prospecting audiences

**Response to fatigue:**
1. New hook (same offer, different opening)
2. New format (static → video or vice versa)
3. New angle (switch from pain to aspiration)
4. New audience segment (fresh eyes reset fatigue)

Do NOT raise budget to fight fatigue — it accelerates the problem.

## Copy Editing Checklist for Ads

- [ ] Does the headline make a specific promise?
- [ ] Is the benefit stated in customer language, not product language?
- [ ] Does the CTA tell them exactly what happens next?
- [ ] Is there one clear message (not three competing claims)?
- [ ] Does it pass the "so what?" test for the ICP?
- [ ] Is any claim unsubstantiated? Remove it or add proof.

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Let's show multiple benefits in the headline" | One promise beats three promises. The brain discards overwhelm. |
| "Our ads need to look polished and professional" | UGC-style raw content consistently outperforms polished creative for cold prospecting. |
| "This creative is working, let's keep running it" | All creative fatigues. Proactively retire and replace before metrics drop. |
| "We'll test 10 variants at once" | Without a testing matrix, you can't isolate what's driving results. Test one dimension at a time. |
| "The product features are the message" | Features are proof. Benefits are the message. Customers buy outcomes, not specs. |
