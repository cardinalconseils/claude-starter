# Content Strategy

Expert knowledge for planning, prioritizing, and executing a content program that drives organic traffic, builds authority, and converts readers into customers.

## Core Framework: The Topic Cluster Model

Modern content strategy uses topic clusters, not disconnected posts.

**Structure:**
- **Pillar page:** Comprehensive, authoritative guide on a broad topic (2,000-5,000+ words)
- **Cluster content:** Narrower posts that cover subtopics in depth, internally linking to the pillar
- **Internal links:** Every cluster page links to the pillar; pillar links to each cluster

**Why it works:**
1. Search engines see topical authority, not just individual page authority
2. Users navigate a coherent knowledge structure (longer sessions, lower bounce)
3. PageRank flows through internal links from cluster → pillar → ranking boost

**Pillar selection criteria:**
- High search volume (> 5,000 searches/month) for the core keyword
- Directly relevant to your ICP's decision-making process
- Your product solves the core problem in the pillar topic
- You can realistically produce 8-15 cluster posts around it

## Step 1: Audience Intent Mapping

Before choosing topics, map your audience's intent at each stage.

| Stage | Intent | Content Type |
|---|---|---|
| Unaware | Learning about a problem they have | Educational guides, "what is X" content |
| Problem-aware | Researching solutions to a known problem | How-to guides, comparison content |
| Solution-aware | Evaluating specific products | Review content, case studies, comparison pages |
| Product-aware | Comparing you vs competitors | Vs pages, feature deep-dives, ROI content |
| Customer | Getting more value from your product | Knowledge base, advanced guides, use case posts |

Most content strategies over-invest in top-of-funnel (unaware) and under-invest in bottom-of-funnel (solution-aware, product-aware). Bottom-of-funnel content converts at 5-10× the rate.

## Step 2: Content Audit (for existing sites)

Before creating new content, audit what you have.

**Audit categories:**
- **Keep + optimize:** Good content that ranks but could be improved
- **Update:** Outdated content that still has potential
- **Merge:** Multiple thin posts on the same topic — consolidate into one strong post
- **Remove:** Content that attracts no traffic and serves no purpose (thin pages, old press releases)

**Audit signals:**
- Organic traffic (Google Search Console)
- Ranking position for target keyword
- Backlinks (Ahrefs or Semrush)
- Conversion rate (goal completions in analytics)

**Content scoring formula:**
```
Content Score = (Monthly organic sessions × 0.4)
              + (Primary keyword ranking position × -0.3)  ← lower = better
              + (Backlinks × 0.2)
              + (Conversion events × 0.1)
```

Use this to prioritize: update high-potential low-performers before creating new content.

## Step 3: Keyword Research & Prioritization

**Keyword research process:**
1. Seed keywords: List the 5-10 core concepts your product relates to
2. Expand: Use Ahrefs, Semrush, or Google Keyword Planner to find variations and related terms
3. Filter: Remove keywords outside your target audience's intent
4. Prioritize: Score by business value × difficulty ratio

**Prioritization matrix:**

| Criteria | Weight | Score (1-10) |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly search volume | 25% | |
| Business relevance (ICP fit) | 35% | |
| Keyword difficulty (inverted) | 25% | |
| CTA / conversion fit | 15% | |

High business relevance beats high search volume. A 500/month keyword that converts at 5% beats a 10,000/month keyword that converts at 0.1%.

## Step 4: Content Pillar Design

**Choosing pillars:**
- 3-5 pillars for most SaaS companies at Pilot/Candidate stage
- Each pillar = one core use case or ICP pain point
- Each pillar should have at least 10 plausible cluster topics

**Pillar examples for SaaS:**
- [Product category] for [ICP type]
- How to [achieve core outcome your product enables]
- [Core process your product automates]: The complete guide

**Cluster topic generation:**
From each pillar, generate cluster topics by asking:
- What questions does someone have before they can implement this pillar topic?
- What are the subtopics someone would want to explore further?
- What tools, comparisons, or templates relate to this pillar?

## Step 5: Editorial Calendar Design

**Calendar structure:**
- Publish cadence: What's sustainable? 2 posts/week? 4/month?
- Distribution: What ratio of top/middle/bottom funnel?
- Mix: Long-form guides, short tactical posts, data-driven pieces, case studies

**Recommended mix for early-stage SaaS:**
- 40% top-of-funnel (educational, broad keywords)
- 30% middle-of-funnel (how-to, use case, problem-solution)
- 30% bottom-of-funnel (comparison, alternative, ROI, case studies)

Many teams do 80% top-of-funnel and then wonder why content doesn't drive revenue.

## Content Quality Criteria

Not all content is equal. Use this rubric before publishing:

**1. Search intent match:** Does this content match what someone searching this keyword actually wants?
**2. Comprehensiveness:** Does it cover the topic well enough that the reader doesn't need to search again?
**3. Freshness signals:** Is the information current? Are examples and data recent?
**4. Unique angle:** Does this add something that doesn't already exist?
**5. Conversion path:** Is there a clear next step for a reader who's ready to engage?

**Content that fails at any of these should not be published until fixed.**

## Distribution Strategy

Creating content is 50% of the job. Distribution is the other 50%.

**Distribution channels:**
- Email newsletter (highest intent, most direct)
- LinkedIn organic (B2B audiences, especially decision-makers)
- Twitter/X (tech and startup audiences)
- Slack communities (niche but high intent)
- Reddit (specific subreddits — must add genuine value)
- Podcast placement (repurpose blog → podcast guest pitches)
- Content syndication (Medium, Substack, industry publications)

**Content repurposing waterfall:**
1. Long-form blog post (core asset)
2. → LinkedIn carousel (visual summary)
3. → Twitter/X thread (key insights)
4. → Email newsletter section
5. → Short-form video script
6. → Podcast talking points

## Measuring Content ROI

**Traffic metrics (leading indicators):**
- Organic sessions per month
- Keyword ranking improvements
- Pages per session (content quality signal)

**Revenue metrics (lagging indicators):**
- Content-assisted conversions (did they read a post before signing up?)
- Content-influenced pipeline (did a post appear in their journey?)
- Attributed MQLs from organic content

**Reporting cadence:**
- Weekly: Publishing cadence (are we hitting targets?)
- Monthly: Traffic trends and keyword ranking changes
- Quarterly: Content attribution to revenue

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|---|---|
| "We need to write about everything to build authority" | Topical depth beats breadth. 20 posts on one cluster outperform 100 posts on 100 unrelated topics. |
| "More posts = more traffic" | Publishing frequency without quality produces thin content penalties. One great post beats five mediocre ones. |
| "We'll figure out distribution later" | Content without distribution is a tree falling in an empty forest. Distribution is part of the strategy, not an afterthought. |
| "SEO takes too long — let's focus on other channels" | Compounding organic traffic is the best marketing asset you can build. The best time to start was 6 months ago. |
| "Let's cover what we find interesting to write" | Write what your ICP is searching for, not what's interesting to write. Interest follows audience, not the other way. |
