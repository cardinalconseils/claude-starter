# Marketing Ideas & Growth Strategy

Expert knowledge for generating, prioritizing, and validating marketing experiments for SaaS and software products.

## Idea Generation Frameworks

### 1. ICE Scoring (for rapid prioritization)

Score every idea on:
- **Impact:** How much will this move the needle? (1-10)
- **Confidence:** How confident are you this will work? (1-10)
- **Ease:** How easy is this to execute? (1-10)

ICE Score = (I + C + E) / 3

Run highest-scoring ideas first. Reassess after each experiment.

### 2. SCAMPER for Marketing Ideas

Apply to your current marketing approach:

- **S**ubstitute: What if we replaced [channel/tactic] with something entirely different?
- **C**ombine: What two tactics haven't we combined? (e.g., content + community + cold email)
- **A**dapt: What works in adjacent industries that we could adapt?
- **M**odify/Magnify: What would happen if we went 10× bigger on [tactic]?
- **P**ut to other uses: What assets (customer list, content, data) could we use differently?
- **E**liminate: What would happen if we stopped [tactic] entirely and focused elsewhere?
- **R**earrange: What if we changed the order of our funnel?

### 3. Channel Expansion Matrix

Map where your ICP spends time and attention:

| Where | What they do there | Marketing opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | Consume professional content | Organic posts, sponsored content, outreach |
| Slack communities | Ask questions, share tools | Community presence, answer questions |
| Reddit | Research tools, share experiences | Genuine participation, AMA |
| Twitter/X | Follow industry discourse | Thought leadership, product updates |
| YouTube | Watch tutorials, product reviews | Tutorial content, sponsor channels |
| Podcasts | Listen during commute | Sponsorship, guest appearances |
| Email | Read newsletters | Newsletter ads, dedicated sends |
| Product Hunt | Discover new tools | Launch strategy |
| G2/Capterra | Evaluate software | Review acquisition, profile optimization |

For each channel: Is your ICP there? Are competitors there? Can you add value?

## High-Leverage Growth Tactics by Stage

### Early Stage (< 100 customers)

**Manual first, then scale:**
- Founder-led sales: Sell personally and document every objection
- Targeted cold outreach: 20 personalized emails/day beats 1,000 generic
- Community presence: Genuinely help in 2-3 Slack/Discord/Reddit communities
- Partner with adjacent products: Co-market with non-competing tools to same ICP
- App integrations: Build where your ICP already spends time

**Content that punches above weight:**
- Comparison pages (alternative to [competitor])
- Specific use case pages for your ICP's industry
- One definitive guide per quarter (2,000+ words)

### Growth Stage (100-1,000 customers)

**Capitalize on existing traction:**
- Referral program: Give your happiest customers a reason to refer
- Case study machine: 1 case study per month from best customers
- SEO at scale: Topic clusters around your core domain
- Paid acquisition: 1-2 channels with proven unit economics
- Conference/event presence: Where your ICP gathers

**Product-led growth layer:**
- Free tier or trial that delivers the "aha moment"
- In-product sharing and collaboration (viral loops)
- Usage-based email triggers (when to upgrade)

### Scale Stage (1,000+ customers)

**Brand and defensibility:**
- Annual industry report (original research → media coverage)
- Community building (owned audience vs rented)
- Analyst relations (Gartner, Forrester, G2)
- Video content at scale (YouTube + Shorts + tutorials)
- Affiliate and partner programs

## Viral Loop Design

A viral loop is when using your product naturally causes new users to discover it.

**Types of viral loops:**
1. **Collaboration loops:** "Invite your team" — invites sent per new user
2. **Sharing loops:** "Share your [output]" — product used publicly creates impressions
3. **Referral loops:** Incentivized referral — both parties get value
4. **Word-of-mouth loops:** Product creates such a remarkable experience that users evangelize

**Viral coefficient formula:**
```
K = i × c
```
- i = average invites/shares per user
- c = conversion rate of those invites
- K > 1 = viral growth; K < 1 = assisted growth

**Designing your viral loop:**
1. Identify the natural sharing moment in your product
2. Make sharing frictionless (one click, pre-populated message)
3. Make the shared output valuable to the recipient
4. Give the referrer a reason to share (credit, status, money)

## Product-Led Growth (PLG) Ideas

PLG uses the product itself as the primary acquisition and conversion channel.

**PLG motion options:**
- **Free trial:** Limited time access to full product (14-30 days)
- **Freemium:** Permanently free tier with upgrade incentives
- **Free tool:** Standalone free tool that leads to the product
- **Open source:** Community version drives enterprise leads

**Free-to-paid conversion levers:**
- Usage limits (you hit the cap → upgrade)
- Collaboration features (invite team → team pays)
- Advanced features gate (premium features visible but locked)
- Integrations gate (connect to CRM → paid tier)

**PLG activation milestones:**
Define the actions that correlate with conversion and retention, then optimize for them:
1. Signup
2. First key action (the "aha moment")
3. Habitual usage (3+ sessions in week 1)
4. Collaboration event (invited a teammate)
5. Upgrade trigger (hit a limit or saw a premium feature)

## Partnership & Co-Marketing Ideas

**Integration partnerships:**
- Build integrations with the tools your ICP already uses
- Get listed in their marketplace/app directory
- Co-create content ("How to use [Product A] + [Product B] together")

**Audience-sharing partnerships:**
- Guest posts on partner blogs
- Cross-promotion in email newsletters
- Co-hosted webinars (split the lead list)
- Joint research reports

**Reseller/affiliate programs:**
- Consultants and agencies who serve your ICP
- Influencers in your niche
- Complementary SaaS companies (with revenue share)

## Idea Validation Before Building

Before investing in a new marketing idea:

**The lean validation test:**
1. Define success metric (reply rate, conversion rate, CAC)
2. Set a minimum threshold ("If we don't hit X in 30 days, we stop")
3. Run the smallest possible version
4. Measure against threshold
5. Scale or kill

**Common validation shortcuts:**
- Cold email to your ICP before building a landing page
- LinkedIn post testing a message before writing the full article
- Manual process before automation (do it by hand to see if it converts)
- Concierge onboarding before building the self-serve flow

## Channels to Stop / Reduce

Just as important as ideas to try: ideas to cut.

**Stop signals:**
- CAC is > 3× LTV for the channel
- You've run the channel for 90+ days without hitting your conversion threshold
- The channel requires constant manual attention without scaling
- You're doing it because competitors do it, not because it works for you

**The one-channel focus test:** If you could only use one marketing channel for the next 90 days, which would it be? That's your primary. Don't dilute it with 10 experiments simultaneously.

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|---|---|
| "We need to be on every channel" | Three channels done well beat seven done poorly. Pick and commit. |
| "This idea worked for [famous company] — let's do it" | Context matters. Their audience, stage, and brand are different. Validate before copying. |
| "Let's build a viral loop first" | Viral loops amplify existing traction. Without a base, K-factor math doesn't help you. |
| "We should focus on brand awareness before conversions" | Brand without conversion measurement is a money pit. Track every channel to outcome. |
| "Marketing is about creativity — we don't need frameworks" | Creativity generates ideas. Frameworks prioritize them. You need both. |
