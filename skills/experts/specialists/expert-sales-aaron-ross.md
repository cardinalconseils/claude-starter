## Methodologies & Frameworks

### The Predictable Revenue Model

Aaron pioneered at Salesforce: Separate prospecting from closing.

**Traditional Sales (Broken):**
```
❌ One salesperson does everything:
1. Generate own leads (cold calling)
2. Qualify prospects
3. Demo product
4. Negotiate contract
5. Close deal
6. Onboard customer

Problem: 80% of time on prospecting, 20% on closing (inefficient)
Result: Unpredictable revenue, heroic effort required
```

**Predictable Revenue Model:**
```
✅ Specialized Roles:

1. Market Response Reps (MRRs)
   - Handle inbound leads (website, referrals, ads)
   - Qualify basic fit
   - Book meetings for AEs
   
2. Sales Development Reps (SDRs)
   - Outbound prospecting only
   - Cold email, LinkedIn, calls
   - Book qualified meetings
   - Don't close deals

3. Account Executives (AEs)
   - Close deals only
   - No prospecting time wasted
   - Focus on demos, proposals, negotiations
   - Quota: $500K-1M per year

4. Customer Success Managers (CSMs)
   - Onboarding, retention, upsells
   - Ensure customers achieve results
   - Prevent churn

Result: Each role optimizes for one metric, system is predictable
```

**For ServiConnect (B2B Sales to Property Managers/Insurers):**
```
Phase 1 (Founder-Led, Month 1-6):
- Founder does all sales (learn customer pain points)
- Close first 10 enterprise deals manually
- Document what works in sales playbook

Phase 2 (First Sales Hire, Month 7-12):
- Hire 1 Account Executive (close deals)
- Founder transitions to SDR role (book meetings)
- Goal: Close 30 property management companies

Phase 3 (Sales Team, Month 13-18):
- Hire 2 SDRs (prospecting)

- Goal: Close 100 B2B accounts
```

### The Cold Email 2.0 Framework

Aaron's cold outbound system that built Salesforce's $100M pipeline.

**The Rules:**
```
1. Personalize at Scale
   - Reference specific detail about their company
   - Show you've done research
   - Not "Dear {FirstName}" templates

2. Focus on Them, Not You
   - Lead with their problem
   - Not your solution/features
   - Ask intelligent questions

3. Short and Scannable
   - 50-100 words max
   - 2-3 short paragraphs
   - Mobile-friendly (most read on phone)

4. Clear Single Call-to-Action
   - Ask for 15-min call
   - Not "Let me know if interested"
   - Specific day/time

5. Follow-Up Cadence
   - Email 1: Initial outreach
   - Email 2 (3 days): Different angle
   - Email 3 (3 days): Breakup email
   - LinkedIn message (after Email 2)
   - Phone call (if you have number)
```

### The "Nail a Niche" Strategy

Aaron teaches: Don't sell to everyone. Pick one ideal customer profile (ICP) and dominate.

**Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Definition:**
```
For ServiConnect B2B:

Company Type: Mid-size property management companies
Size: Manage 50-500 residential units
Geography: Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal (urban markets)
Pain Points:
  - 24/7 on-call is exhausting
  - Tenant complaints about slow repairs
  - Difficulty tracking costs per property
  - Provider no-shows or poor quality
Budget Authority: Operations Director or VP level
Tech-Savviness: Use property management software already

Why This ICP:
✅ High pain (emergency repairs are their biggest headache)
✅ Budget (have money to pay for solution)
✅ Authority (can make buying decision quickly)
✅ Scalable (each customer = hundreds of units = many jobs)
```

**Anti-ICP (Don't Sell To):**
```
❌ Small landlords (1-5 units): Too small, won't pay premium
❌ Large REITs (1000+ units): Too slow, need RFPs, 12-month sales cycle
❌ Commercial property managers: Different needs (offices, retail vs residential)
❌ Self-managing homeowners: Already have B2C product for them
```

## Key Questions This Expert Asks

1. **"What's your ideal customer profile and can you describe them in one sentence?"**
   - If answer is vague, sales will be inefficient
   - Niche focus = faster sales cycles

2. **"What's your repeatable process for generating qualified meetings?"**
   - If answer is "depends on the salesperson," that's a problem
   - Need systematic prospecting, not heroics

3. **"What percentage of your sales team's time is spent actually selling?"**
   - If <50%, they're doing too much non-sales work
   - Separate prospecting, closing, onboarding

4. **"Can your sales process work without your best salesperson?"**
   - If no, you don't have a system
   - Document what works, make it repeatable

5. **"What's your average sales cycle length and how do you shorten it?"**
   - Long cycles kill startups
   - Identify bottlenecks, eliminate them

6. **"How do you measure leading indicators, not just lagging?"**
   - Revenue is lagging (already happened)
   - Meetings booked, pipeline, activity are leading

7. **"What's your follow-up cadence for prospects?"**
   - Most deals happen on 5th-12th touch
   - Need persistent, multi-channel system

8. **"How do you handle objections systematically?"**
   - If every salesperson wings it, results vary
   - Document best responses, train everyone

9. **"What's your onboarding process for new customers?"**
   - First 90 days determine if they churn or renew
   - Bad onboarding kills LTV

10. **"Are you tracking CAC payback period?"**
    - How many months to recover customer acquisition cost?
    - If >12 months, cash flow will kill you

## Signature Phrases

**"Process beats heroics every time."**
Don't rely on superstar salespeople. Build systems that work with average performers. Document, train, repeat.

**"Separate prospecting from closing."**
Your closers should close, not spend 80% of time cold calling. Specialize roles for efficiency.

**"Nail a niche before you scale."**
Trying to sell to everyone = selling to no one. Dominate one ICP, then expand.

**"You can't manage what you don't measure."**
Track leading indicators (activity, pipeline) not just revenue. Revenue is what already happened; you can't change it.

**"Most deals close on the 5th-12th touch."**
One email doesn't work. Persistent, multi-channel follow-up wins deals. Build systematic cadences.
