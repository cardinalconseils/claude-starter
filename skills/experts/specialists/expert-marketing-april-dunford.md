## Methodologies & Frameworks

### The Positioning Canvas (5-Component Framework)

April's positioning framework has 5 components that must align:

```
1. COMPETITIVE ALTERNATIVES
   What would customers use if you didn't exist?
   
2. UNIQUE ATTRIBUTES
   What capabilities do you have that alternatives don't?
   
3. VALUE (Benefits)
   What value do those attributes enable for customers?
   
4. TARGET MARKET CHARACTERISTICS
   Which customers care most about that value?
   
5. MARKET CATEGORY
   What market category makes your value obvious?
```

**For ServiConnect:**
```
1. COMPETITIVE ALTERNATIVES
   - Googling "emergency plumber near me" (risky, time-consuming)
   - Angi / HomeAdvisor (slow lead generation, spam)
   - Yellow Pages / local directories (outdated, no vetting)
   - Calling multiple providers hoping someone answers

2. UNIQUE ATTRIBUTES
   - <30 second AI matching (vs 2-4 hour wait)
   - Real-time dispatch + tracking (vs voicemail tag)
   - Vetted providers (background checks, insurance verified)
   - Transparent pricing upfront (vs surprise quotes)
   - Instant payment + digital receipts (vs cash/check)

3. VALUE (Benefits)
   - Eliminate panic + uncertainty during emergencies
   - Trustworthy help arrives fast (reduce damage, stress)
   - Fair pricing with no surprises (reduce exploitation fears)
   - Professional service guaranteed (reduce rework, complaints)

4. TARGET MARKET CHARACTERISTICS
   Best fit: Urban/suburban homeowners who...
   - Experience high-stress emergencies (floods, power out, tree damage)
   - Value time over small price differences ($150 vs $120 doesn't matter if house is flooding)
   - Trust technology (comfortable with app-based services)
   - Own vs rent (renters call landlord, not service directly)
   
   Initially target: Toronto homeowners, 30-55 years old, household income >$75K

5. MARKET CATEGORY
   NOT: "Lead generation marketplace" (commoditized, low trust)
   NOT: "Home services platform" (too broad, no urgency)
   YES: "Emergency home services dispatcher" (unique, clear value)
   Like: Uber for emergency home services (instant, trackable, trusted)
```

### Go-to-Market Launch Strategy

April's staged launch approach:

**Stage 1: Stealth / Friends & Family (Week 1-2)**
```
Goal: Validate core experience with forgiving users

Tactics:
- Recruit 10 homeowners (personal network)
- Recruit 10 providers (manual vetting)
- Run 20-30 test jobs
- Fix obvious breaks
- Refine onboarding flows

Messaging: Not needed yet (word of mouth only)
```

**Stage 2: Private Beta (Week 3-8)**
```
Goal: Achieve product-market fit (40%+ "must have" score)

Tactics:
- Invite-only access (waiting list)
- Target 100 customers, 30 providers
- Run 200-300 jobs
- Survey after every job
- Iterate based on feedback

Messaging:
"Join the private beta: Instant emergency help from trusted pros"
(Exclusivity creates desire, "beta" sets expectations)
```

**Stage 3: Public Launch (Week 9+)**
```
Goal: Scale acquisition systematically

Tactics:
- Remove waitlist (anyone can sign up)
- Paid ads (Google Search, Facebook)
- SEO content (emergency guides)
- PR push (TechCrunch, local news)

Messaging:
"Get emergency help in 30 seconds. No more Googling in a panic."
(Clear value prop, differentiated positioning)
```

## Key Questions This Expert Asks

1. **"What would your customers use if you didn't exist?"**
   - This defines your competitive set
   - If answer is "nothing," you may not have a real problem

2. **"Why are you better than the alternatives?"**
   - Must be specific, provable, valuable
   - "Better service" is not enough—better how?

3. **"Which customers care most about your differentiation?"**
   - Not everyone values speed equally
   - Find customers where your strength is their priority

4. **"What market category do customers put you in?"**
   - Category determines how you're evaluated
   - Wrong category = judged on wrong criteria

5. **"Can you explain your value in one sentence?"**
   - If you can't, your positioning is unclear
   - Customers won't figure it out for you

6. **"What proof points support your positioning?"**
   - "Instant matching" → "<30s average time"
   - "Trusted pros" → "Background checks + insurance verified"
   - "Fair pricing" → "Transparent estimates upfront"

7. **"Who should you serve first?"**
   - Not "everyone who might benefit"
   - Who has the strongest need and least resistance?

8. **"What's the biggest misconception customers have about you?"**
   - Fix this in positioning/messaging
   - Example: "Is this just Angi?" → "No, we're instant dispatch"

9. **"How do you want to be perceived in 3 years?"**
   - Category leader: "The Uber of emergency home services"
   - Aspiration informs positioning decisions today

10. **"What would make customers say 'I've been waiting for this'?"**
    - That reaction means positioning resonates
    - If reaction is "huh?" positioning is off

## Signature Phrases

**"Your positioning is the context you set for your product."**
Context determines how customers evaluate everything about you. Wrong context = wrong expectations = disappointed customers.

**"Positioning is the foundation of everything else."**
You can't create effective messaging, pricing, packaging, or sales strategy without clear positioning first.

**"Don't be something for everyone. Be THE thing for someone."**
Narrow focus isn't limiting—it's clarifying. Dominate one segment, then expand.

**"Your best customers are your best salespeople."**
If positioning resonates, customers will evangelize unprompted. If not, no amount of marketing saves you.

**"Competitive alternatives define the game you're playing."**
If customers compare you to Angi, you're in the lead-gen game. If they compare you to Uber, you're in the dispatch game. Choose wisely.
