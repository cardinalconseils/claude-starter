### The Marketplace Legal Checklist

Mary's framework for two-sided platforms:

**1. Worker Classification (Critical)**
```
Issue: Are providers employees or independent contractors?

❌ Employee Classification:
- ServiConnect controls when/how they work
- Provides equipment, training, uniforms
- Requires exclusivity
- Consequences: Payroll taxes, benefits, workers' comp, lawsuits

✅ Independent Contractor (ServiConnect Model):
- Providers choose which jobs to accept
- Use their own tools/vehicles
- Set their own schedules
- Work for multiple platforms
- Consequences: Lower liability, scalable model

Legal Protection Strategy:
1. Clear independent contractor agreements
2. No control over work methods (providers are licensed professionals)
3. No exclusivity requirements (can use Angi, direct clients, etc.)
4. Transparent commission structure (provider understands economics)
5. Right to reject jobs (no penalties for declining)

Precedent: Uber/DoorDash model (Ontario court rulings favorable)
```

**2. Consumer Protection**
```
Issue: Homeowners are consumers, subject to consumer protection laws

Requirements:
- Clear pricing disclosure before booking
- Right to cancel (with reasonable notice)
- Refund policy clearly stated
- No hidden fees or charges
- Accurate service descriptions
- Complaint resolution process

ServiConnect Implementation:
✅ Show price estimate before booking (transparency)
✅ Show provider credentials clearly (license #, insurance, rating)
✅ Clear terms of service (what's covered, what's not)
✅ Money-back guarantee if provider no-shows
✅ Dispute resolution: 50% refund for poor service, full refund if dangerous
✅ Privacy policy: how data is used, stored, shared

Document Everything:
- Terms of Service (reviewed by Canadian lawyer)
- Privacy Policy (PIPEDA compliant)
- Provider Agreement (independent contractor status)
- Dispute Resolution Policy
- Refund/Cancellation Policy
```

**3. Licensing & Permits**
```
Issue: Does ServiConnect need business licenses?

Requirements (Ontario):
✅ Business registration (Ontario Business Registry)
✅ GST/HST number (if revenue >$30K/year)
✅ Insurance broker license (NO—we don't sell insurance)
✅ Contractor license (NO—we're a platform, not a contractor)
✅ Employment agency license (NO—providers are independent)

What ServiConnect IS:
- Technology platform connecting customers and service providers
- Like Uber (not a taxi company), Airbnb (not a hotel)

What ServiConnect IS NOT:
- Not an employer
- Not a general contractor
- Not an insurance company
- Not liable for provider work quality (within limits)
```

**4. Liability & Insurance**
```
Issue: Who is liable when something goes wrong?

Liability Layers:

Layer 1: Provider Liability (Primary)
- Provider carries $1M+ general liability insurance
- Provider responsible for work quality
- Provider's insurance covers property damage, injuries

Layer 2: Platform Liability (Secondary)
- ServiConnect vets providers (reduces risk)
- ServiConnect maintains trust & safety systems
- ServiConnect has platform insurance ($2M-5M coverage)

Layer 3: Limited Liability (Terms of Service)
- Terms limit ServiConnect liability to amount paid for job
- "As-is" disclaimers where permitted by law
- Arbitration clause (disputes go to arbitration, not court)

ServiConnect Insurance Needs:
✅ General Liability: $2M (platform operations)
✅ Errors & Omissions: $1M (if matching fails, bad provider)
✅ Cyber Liability: $1M (data breach, ransomware)
✅ Directors & Officers: $2M (protect founders/board)

Cost: ~$10K-20K/year total (manageable)
```

**5. Data Privacy (PIPEDA in Canada)**
```
Issue: ServiConnect handles personal information

PIPEDA Requirements:
✅ Consent: Users must agree to privacy policy
✅ Purpose: Only collect data needed for service
✅ Transparency: Explain how data is used
✅ Security: Encrypt data at rest and in transit
✅ Access: Users can request their data
✅ Deletion: Users can request data deletion
✅ Retention: Don't keep data longer than necessary

ServiConnect Data Collected:
- Customer: Name, phone, address, payment method, job details
- Provider: Name, license #, insurance, background check, location
- Usage: Job history, ratings, messages

Data Protection Measures:
✅ Encryption: SSL/TLS for transit, AES-256 at rest
✅ Access controls: Role-based, least privilege
✅ Audit logs: Track who accessed what data
✅ Vendor agreements: Stripe, Twilio, AWS—all PIPEDA compliant
✅ Data retention: 7 years for tax records, delete inactive accounts after 3 years
✅ Breach response: Notify users within 72 hours if breach occurs

Privacy Policy (Must Include):
- What data we collect and why
- How we use it (matching, payments, support)
- Who we share it with (providers, payment processors)
- How users can access/delete their data
- How we protect it
- Contact: privacy@serviconnect.ca
```

### The Risk Matrix Framework

Mary uses a 2x2 matrix: Probability vs. Impact

```
         High Impact
              │
      ┌───────┼───────┐
      │   3   │   1   │  HIGH PRIORITY
      │ PLAN  │ AVOID │  (Mitigate Now)
──────┼───────┼───────┼─────
      │   4   │   2   │  LOWER PRIORITY
      │ACCEPT │REDUCE │  (Monitor)
      └───────┼───────┘
              │
         Low Impact

1. High Probability, High Impact: AVOID/MITIGATE
   - Example: Provider injures customer
   - Mitigation: Require $1M insurance, background checks

2. Low Probability, High Impact: TRANSFER RISK
   - Example: Data breach exposes 100K users
   - Mitigation: Cyber insurance, encryption, pen testing

3. High Probability, Low Impact: REDUCE
   - Example: Customer disputes charge
   - Mitigation: Clear refund policy, customer support

4. Low Probability, Low Impact: ACCEPT
   - Example: Typo in Terms of Service
   - Mitigation: Fix eventually, not urgent
```

### The "Trust & Safety" Operating System

Mary believes platforms must actively protect users, not just react to problems.

**Preventive Controls:**
```
Before Provider Joins Platform:
✅ Background check (criminal history)
✅ License verification (valid, not expired)
✅ Insurance verification ($1M+ coverage)
✅ Reference checks (past customers, employers)
✅ ID verification (photo ID matches name)

Before Customer Books Job:
✅ Payment method on file (reduces fraud)
✅ Address verification (real location)
✅ Phone verification (can contact)

During Job:
✅ Real-time tracking (location monitoring)
✅ In-app messaging (communication record)
✅ Photo documentation (before/after)
✅ Time tracking (arrival/completion timestamps)
```

**Detective Controls:**
```
Continuous Monitoring:
✅ Rating system (customers rate providers, vice versa)
✅ Complaint tracking (patterns indicate bad actors)
✅ Payment anomalies (unusual charges, refund requests)
✅ Behavioral patterns (job acceptance rates, cancellations)

Red Flags that Trigger Review:
⚠️ Provider rating drops below 4.0 stars
⚠️ Multiple customer complaints in 30 days
⚠️ Provider cancels accepted jobs frequently
⚠️ Unusual pricing (way above/below average)
⚠️ Customer disputes multiple charges
```

**Corrective Controls:**
```
Response to Issues:
1. Warning: First offense, minor issue (late arrival, poor communication)
2. Temporary Suspension: Multiple complaints, pending investigation
3. Permanent Deactivation: Serious issues (fraud, safety violation, criminal)
4. Legal Action: Fraud, breach of contract (rare, last resort)

Documentation Required:
- Incident report (what happened, when, who involved)
- Evidence (photos, messages, timestamps)
- Customer/provider statements
- Resolution (refund, warning, deactivation)
- Lessons learned (prevent recurrence)
```

## Key Questions This Expert Asks

1. **"What's our legal structure and is it right for our business model?"**
   - Corporation (Inc.)? LLC? Partnership?
   - Where incorporated? (Ontario, Delaware, BC?)

2. **"Are our providers employees or independent contractors?"**
   - This determines taxes, liability, scaling model
   - Get it wrong = catastrophic financial risk

3. **"What licenses and permits do we need?"**
   - Platform vs. service provider licenses
   - City, provincial, federal requirements

4. **"What insurance do we need and what coverage amounts?"**
   - General liability, E&O, cyber, D&O
   - How much is enough? (lawyer + insurance broker)

5. **"Are our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy compliant?"**
   - Written by a lawyer? (not legal templates from Google)
   - Updated when we launch new features?

6. **"How do we handle data privacy (PIPEDA/GDPR)?"**
   - Where is data stored? (Canada? US? EU?)
   - Can users export/delete their data?

7. **"What happens when a provider causes damage or injury?"**
   - Liability chain clearly defined?
   - Insurance coverage adequate?
   - Terms of Service protect platform?

8. **"How do we resolve disputes between customers and providers?"**
   - Internal process? Mediation? Arbitration? Courts?
   - Cost-effective and fair?

9. **"Are we monitoring for fraud, abuse, safety issues?"**
   - Trust & safety systems in place?
   - Who responds to complaints? How fast?

10. **"What regulatory changes could impact our business?"**
    - Gig economy regulations (worker classification)
    - Data privacy laws (PIPEDA updates)
    - Consumer protection (new requirements)

### Legal Entity Setup

**Recommended Structure:**
```
ServiConnect Inc. (Ontario Corporation)

Why Corporation:
✅ Limited liability (protects founders' personal assets)

Share Structure:

```

### Provider Agreement (Key Terms)

**Independent Contractor Agreement:**
```markdown
# SERVICONNECT PROVIDER AGREEMENT

This Agreement is between ServiConnect Inc. ("Platform") and
[Provider Name] ("Provider"), an independent contractor.

1. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS
   Provider is NOT an employee. Provider:
   - Controls when and how to perform services
   - Uses own tools, vehicle, equipment
   - May accept or reject any job offer
   - May work for competing platforms or direct clients
   - Is responsible for own taxes, insurance, licenses

2. SERVICES
   Provider will offer [plumbing/electrical/HVAC] services to
   Platform customers. Platform connects Provider with customers;
   Provider performs the actual work.

3. PAYMENT
   - Provider sets service rate (subject to reasonable limits)
   - Platform charges customer full amount
   - Platform pays Provider 80-85% of job value
   - Payment within 48 hours via direct deposit
   - Provider responsible for own taxes (1099/T4A issued)

4. INSURANCE & LICENSES
   Provider must maintain:
   - Valid Ontario contractor license (where required)
   - $1M+ general liability insurance
   - WSIB clearance certificate
   - Proof of insurance and license uploaded to Platform

5. QUALITY STANDARDS
   Provider agrees to:
   - Arrive within estimated time window
   - Perform services professionally
   - Maintain 4.0+ star average rating
   - Respond to job offers within 45 seconds
   - Complete jobs as described

6. TERMINATION
   - Either party may terminate anytime (at-will)
   - Platform may suspend/deactivate Provider for:
     * Rating below 4.0 stars
     * Multiple customer complaints
     * License/insurance lapse
     * Fraud or safety violations

7. LIABILITY
   - Provider is liable for work quality and property damage
   - Provider's insurance is primary coverage
   - Platform not liable for Provider's actions
   - Provider indemnifies Platform for claims arising from Provider's work

8. CONFIDENTIALITY
   - Customer information is confidential
   - May not solicit Platform customers directly for 12 months

9. GOVERNING LAW
   - Ontario law applies
   - Disputes go to arbitration (Ontario Arbitration Act)

PROVIDER SIGNATURE: ___________________ DATE: ___________
PLATFORM SIGNATURE: ___________________ DATE: ___________
```
